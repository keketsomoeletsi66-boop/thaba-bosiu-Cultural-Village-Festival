An oligopoly demand curve is the demand curve faced by a firm in an oligopoly market structure (a market with a few large firms). It shows how much quantity a firm can sell at different prices, considering the reactions of rival firms. Ansari(2024) added that in an oligopoly demand curve,there is no straight line it has different values of price elasticity of demand above and below the kink (bend). The kinked demand curve explains price rigidity and the interdependent behaviour of firms under non-collusive models of oligopoly. Assumptions of the FIRMS ANTICIPATE COMPETITORS TO MATCH PRICE DECREASE BUT NOT PRICE INCREASES: In an oligopoly, firms believe competitors will react differently to price increases and price decreases. When a firm lowers its price, competitors firms are likely to follow in order to avoid losing customers, which makes demand relatively inelastic below the current price. However, when a firm increases its price, other firms may not follow, causing customers to switch to cheaper alternatives, making demand relatively elastic above the current price. This difference in reaction creates a kink in the demand curve at the current price level. According to Open stax(2022) one things of things that makes an oligopoly demand curve, to have a kink it that: Even when oligopolists recognize that they would benefit as a group by acting like a monopoly, each individual oligopoly faces a private temptation to produce just a slightly higher quantity and earn slightly higher profit—while still counting on the other oligopolists to hold down their production and keep prices high, therefore asymmetric competitor reactions lead directly to the kinked shape of the demand curve in oligopoly. kinked demand curve explains why prices tend to remain stable (rigid) in oligopolistic markets. Firms fear losing market share when increasing prices and gaining little advantage when decreasing prices, they are discouraged from changing prices. Even if costs change slightly, firms often maintain the same price due to uncertainty about competitors’ reactions.
